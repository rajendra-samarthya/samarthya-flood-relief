About this dashboard
This is the verified impact dashboard for Samarthya's emergency flood relief response following the September 2025 flooding in Osmanabad (Dharashiv) district, Marathwada, Maharashtra.
The dashboard presents distribution data for 3,000 beneficiary households across 55 habitations in Omerga and Lohara blocks — with GPS-tagged, photo-verified, and signature-acknowledged records for every single distribution.

The flood — September 2025
In September 2025, Osmanabad (Dharashiv) district received 830mm of rainfall — 148% above average. The Bennitura, Manjra, and Sinha rivers overflowed. 22 of 48 mandals were severely affected. Over 270 villages were impacted, 766 houses damaged, 186 cattle lost, and more than 300 people were rescued by emergency teams.
The communities hit hardest were De-notified and Nomadic Tribe (DNT) households — Laman Banjara, Wadar, Berad, and Mariaaiwale — living in remote tandas and vastis, historically excluded from mainstream relief systems.

Communities served
The relief operation specifically targeted De-notified and Nomadic Tribe (DNT) communities:
Laman Banjara — 81.98% of PDM respondents
Wadar — 9.01%
Berad / Bedar — 6.31%
Mariaaiwale — 2.70%
DNT communities are among the most structurally excluded groups in Maharashtra — landless, seasonally mobile, and largely absent from government beneficiary lists. Samarthya has worked with these communities since 2008.

Accountability standards
This operation was implemented in accordance with the Core Humanitarian Standard (CHS) 2024 edition. Evidence for all 9 commitments was collected through:
Pre-distribution baseline survey (1,067 households)
Digital distribution records via KoboToolbox (3,000 records)
Post-Distribution Monitoring survey (111 households, 12 FGDs)
Suchna Peti (community feedback boxes) at every distribution point

About Samarthya
Samarthya Kalyankari Sanstha is a women-led, youth-initiated grassroots nonprofit based in Osmanabad (Dharashiv), Maharashtra. Working since 2008 across 50 villages in Omerga and Lohara blocks, Samarthya runs five programmes:
Programme	Focus
Bharari	Gender justice
Saksham Shikshan	Quality education
Umed	Youth and citizenship
Swayampurn	Entitlement and resilience
Madat	Relief and rebuilding
Statutory compliance: FCRA · 12A · 80G · CSR-1
Governance: Code of Conduct · Safeguarding Policy · POSH · Do No Harm framework
